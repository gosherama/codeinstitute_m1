Meet The Monkees

In 1965, over 400 people responded to an ad seeking young men for a new television show about a rock group called The Monkees. The Monkees premiered on September 12th, 1966. Some of their best-loved and number one hits included Neil Diamond's "I'm a Believer" and "Last Train to Clarksville".

More

The Monkees are an American rock and pop band (1966-1971) formed in Los Angeles in 1965 for the American TV series "The Monkees" (1966-1968). The musical acting quartet was composed of Americans Micky Dolenz, Michael Nesmith, and Peter Tork; and British actor and singer Davy Jones. Nesmith did compose and produce some songs from the beginning, and Peter Tork contributed limited guitar work on the sessions produced by Nesmith. They eventually fought for the right to collectively supervise all musical output under the band's name. The television show was canceled in 1968, but the band continued to record music through 1971.
